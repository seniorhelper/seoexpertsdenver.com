Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Denver SEO

What does an SEO expert actually do?

An SEO expert researches your market, audits your website, identifies technical and content issues, and builds a strategy to improve your rankings. This includes fixing structural problems that prevent search engines from crawling your site, creating content that answers what your customers are searching for, strengthening local signals like your Google Business Profile, and structuring everything to be visible in AI-powered search tools as well.

How long does SEO take to show results in Denver?

Most businesses see meaningful improvement in 3 to 6 months. Local SEO — like map pack rankings — can move faster, sometimes within 6 to 8 weeks. Broader or more competitive keywords take longer. The timeline depends on how much foundational work is needed and how consistently the strategy is executed. Want a more specific estimate? Request a free audit and we'll give you an honest assessment for your situation.

What is the difference between SEO and GEO?

Traditional SEO focuses on ranking in Google's standard blue-link results. Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) focuses on getting your business cited in the answers AI tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity generate. Both matter now. SEO gets you found. GEO gets you recommended. We optimize for both simultaneously.

How much does SEO cost in Denver?

SEO pricing varies based on scope of work, market competitiveness, and the level of ongoing involvement needed. Monthly retainers for Denver businesses typically range from a few hundred dollars for basic local campaigns to several thousand for comprehensive strategies. We focus on transparent pricing and ROI mindset — not on locking clients into packages that don't match their actual needs.

Why should I choose SEO over paid ads?

Paid ads deliver fast visibility but stop the moment you pause spending. SEO builds an asset — authority and rankings that compound over time. Many Denver businesses find that combining both works well early on, then shifting more weight to SEO as organic traffic grows. Read our full SEO vs paid ads breakdown.
